Research Academic Output

Summary and self-assessment

This year much of my research output was based on the past few years of designing software and methods around longitudinal and asynchronous biorepositories. I served on a panel at a Cancer informatics leadership meeting, had two abstracts accepted to the AMIA Joint Summits on Translational Science as a podium talk and peer guided workshop respectively.   I was also invited to review abstracts for the same conference and this coming year's AMIA symposium where I reviewed two papers and an abstract.  As a highlight of the year, I submitted my first paper to a conference.
In my second year I would like to work closer with my advisor and find the integration points between my work at CHOP and my studies at Drexel. I hope to find publication routes with my advisor for this work.
