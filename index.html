Empowering heterogeneous data driven resources to fuel translational research in rare pediatric cancer

Abstract— Every day we collaborate on social media and productivity applications, which communicate over web services. These communications are full of heterogeneous data (different file formats, technical syntax, size, etc.) These data are compared with other data through algorithmic population-based decisions and transformed into information that is served in an application of a user’s choice, anytime, anywhere. As strides in information technology are making connectivity and obtaining powerful information easier, technology is also driving the biomedical domain and pushing boundaries in medical research. One of the most exciting advances happened just over ten years ago: A large collaborative effort led to the unraveling of the entire human genome sequence. This discovery quickly unlocked connections between our genetic code and our health profile. Today, our ability to unlock the information in a cell through advanced DNA and RNA sequencing goes beyond merely our inherited genomic traits to include a complex understanding of the molecular underpinnings and functions of one of humanity’s greatest foes: cancer.

Biologically based cancer research requires a combination of significant amounts of physical tumor tissue, a full understanding of a patient’s phenotype (e.g. their medical history) and access to large genomic data sets derived from both the tumor and the patient. These data sets are dimensionally diverse in size, scope and platform, but they must be taken as a whole to discover molecular pathways and create new classifications of disease to impact clinical outcomes (e.g. responses to targeted therapies and survival).

This prospectus explores the modern, semantic data driven web paradigm for biomedical researchers who study rare pediatric brain tumors. It tackles, trough evaluation of translational informatics platforms, data driven integrated infrastructure for researchers needing to operationalize outside the walls of one institution to establish critical mass of information and specimens then query the resulting heterogeneous data in connected applications. As a potential area of dissertation research, data mining and machine learning techniques are compared to evaluate the utility in supplementing data in the rare tumor databases with large published data sets readily available in heavily researched areas of cancer and observational health networks. This could fill an identified void in biologically based cancer research where there is simply not enough clinical annotation of rare disease repositories, particularly in pediatrics. This research can empower data use and access to drive discovery for a population in need of new and novel therapies.


Scalable Biobanking: A Modular Electronic Honest Broker and Biorepository for Integrated Clinical, Specimen and Genomic Research

A Data Driven and Integrative Approach to Asychronous, Longitudinal and Collaborative Specimen and Genomic Research

Abstract— Biorepository research has introduced significant challenges to biomedical informatics systems design and implementation. We take a best-of-breed system integrative approach to finding solutions to administer a biobank operationally, use a biobank scientifically and integrate clinical, specimen and genomic data. We introduce an electronic Honest Broker (eHB) and Biorepository Portal (BRP) open source project that, in tandem, allow for integration of data on the input and output while protecting subject privacy, a primary issue in research and healthcare informatics. Research data management systems like REDCap and our proprietary laboratory information management system (LIMS) are used as best-of- breed client systems and integrated with downstream genomic analyses. Taking this honest brokered system-client based, modular approach to biorepository research, we allow for data and specimens to be associated with a biorepository subject at anytime point asynchronously, lowering the bar to develop new research projects based on scientific merit and having a future proofed specimen set for collaborative advanced genomic and tissue research yet to be established.

FACT SHEET: Obama Administration Announces Key Actions to Accelerate Precision Medicine Initiative

In conjunction with today’s announcements, more than 40 private-sector organizations are making commitments that will accelerate precision medicine, in alignment with the key principles of the Precision Medicine Initiative

The Children's Hospital of Philadelphia—is announcing the launch of a new Center for Data-Driven Discovery in Biomedicine (D3b). Working with leading hospitals, industry partners, foundations, patients, and their families, the Center will develop a multi-institutional biorepository infrastructure and data-analysis ecosystem that supports patient participation in collaborative pediatric cancer tissue-based research and a shared, open-data infrastructure that will drive precision medicine discovery. Integrated initiatives will support patients’ access, use, and sharing of their own genomic and clinical data. These platforms, which are undergoing comprehensive development in 2016, will help doctors match cancer therapies to individual children and define new precision medicine approaches for pediatric cancer and childhood diseases.
